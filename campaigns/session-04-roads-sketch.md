## SHARED CANON *(true on every road)*

### The three failed anchor-points *(Yelvyne's journal, the last six months)*

| Anchor | Where | Reached by |
|---|---|---|
| **Thornmere** | mill village, two days south past Aldermarch | South road |
| **Sallow Ness** | sea-lantern on the headlands past Gullwash | West road |
| **Farrowfield** | fair-green village; the wandering market was camped there the week its lantern died | The market |

Every road leads to one. Player agency picks the door; the spine is the same.

### The cipher (Nya's page) — what it actually says

- **It is not magic.** Detect Magic finds *nothing* — play that as a character beat for Nya, not
  a dead end. Comprehend Languages fails too (RAW: the spell doesn't decode ciphers). Only a
  reader who knows the hand can break it — and the hand is **Lantern-Circle liturgical
  shorthand.** 🔒 The second reveal hiding under the first: *whoever wrote this was trained by
  the Circle.* Halve has Circle history. Never explain it — let a sister recognize the hand and
  go quiet. 🔒
- **Decoded, the page is an itinerary.** A column of place-names, dated. **Three struck
  through:** Thornmere. Sallow Ness. Farrowfield. Then **Hollowmere — not struck** — annotated
  with a sigil the sisters read as *"witnessed — contested."* Below it the list continues, and
  the page is **torn.** The rest of his route exists. They just don't have it.
- **A margin note, repeated like a prayer:** *"Not cut. Agreed. It must be agreed."*
- **A tally in the corner: five small strokes.** 🔒 The witnesses. Let the table count
  themselves. Say nothing. 🔒

### Yelvyne's sisters *(which one they meet depends on the road)*

- **Sister SEFRA** — Aldermarch; runs the lantern-hostel by the south gate. Brisk, urban, keeps
  ledgers — the Circle's road-network administrator. Decodes fully and fast. Also the one
  tracking the **Drow-blame** rumors moving up the Silverway.
- **Sister MAREN** — Gullwash; tends the ruin of Sallow Ness, the anchor she couldn't save.
  Weathered, half-deaf from the wind, guilt she doesn't perform. Decodes slowly (older hand).
  First person to say the tide-song is *words.*
- **QUILL** — a *lapsed* sister trading in the wandering market: lantern-charms and "one true
  answer per customer." Decodes for a price — not gold: **one truth the party has never said
  aloud.** 🔒 She recognizes the hand instantly: *"I knew a man who wrote like this. He
  recruited, too."* She left the Circle over something Halve-shaped. Hook only — don't spend
  it yet. 🔒

## 🛣 SOUTH — THE SILVERWAY *(the Halve thread · Thane's road)*

**THE RIDE.** One long day to Aldermarch. Thane's brass needle holds steady down the road
(Aldermarch's anchor) — the first proof the compass isn't broken, if anyone's watching.

**THE SET-PIECE — the Waystone.** A lantern-inn at the halfway ford. Among the guests: a
nervous "wool merchant," **CORVIN** — actually a defected *ferrier* from Halve's network,
carrying route-papers he wants to trade for protection. At dusk, **bounty toughs** arrive
(4–6 thug/bandit mix — tune it in the encounter builder), hired out of Aldermarch on the
Drow-blame rumors, hunting "the drow witch and her accomplices." They don't know it's
Sarranthia's party — social first, steel if it breaks. **If Corvin lives:** he recognizes the
cipher's handwriting — *"That's how* he *writes"* — knows the network runs on ferriers and
dead-drops, and 🔒 has heard the name **Cael** spoken as *"an asset that got kept."* Do NOT
connect it to Thane yet. Plant only. 🔒

**THE SISTER & THE DECODE.** Sefra, at the south-gate hostel. Full decode over one evening.
Warns the party the Drow-blame is organized, not just gossip — someone is paying for it.

**THE ANCHOR CLUE — Thornmere, two days on.** An emptied town. No monsters, no bodies — just
*absence*, and a shrine that stands intact but hollow. *"Not broken. Agreed away."* Their first
look at what losing actually costs.

**SPOTLIGHTS.** Thane (compass + Corvin's plant) · Sarranthia (Drow-blame heat — danger beats
with one kindness counterbeat) · Creed (a **House Ashmantle factor** in Aldermarch asks him
quiet questions — what does the House want with severances?).

**MAPS TO BUY/PREP.** Forest road + river-ford waystation inn (day and night), Aldermarch
gate/market street, abandoned village green (Thornmere).

## 🌊 WEST — THE COAST *(Willa's road · the drowned city)*

**THE RIDE.** A day and a half of cliff path. At night the tide's singing is *audible* — a
chord under the water. If Willa flies it as the gull: 🔒 at slack tide she sees **lights
beneath the surface.** Escalate her visions here. 🔒

**THE SET-PIECE — the Nightwake.** A Gullwash fishing crew rowed out to "follow the song."
Their boat drifts back at dusk — crew standing, breathing, **drowned-quiet**: salt-blank eyes,
humming the chord, walking off the deck toward the Ness. Not corrupted-fey wrong; *older*
wrong. They aren't evil — they're entranced. **Subdue, don't kill** — the table has practice
from Petal, and they'll feel it rhyme. 🔒 Behind it: something at Sallow Ness is using the
failed anchor as a *mouth*. The drowned city's bond predates the Circle's — this is the first
breadcrumb. 🔒

**THE SISTER & THE DECODE.** Maren, at the Sallow Ness ruin. Slow decode across a night. Her
guilt corroborates Yelvyne's journal — she watched her anchor die and couldn't say why.

**THE ANCHOR CLUE — Sallow Ness itself.** The great lantern-glass is *gone* — salvaged.
Someone is stripping the dead anchor-sites. (Braids straight into the market's relic-stall.)

**SPOTLIGHTS.** Willa (home waters; rumor that **Edrik Thorne's ship was seen running dark,
no lanterns** — hook only) · Nya (a face she knows on the Gullwash docks — plant only, guilds
live in ports) · Creed (fisherfolk ask the dragon-knight to bless the boats — pure warmth).

**MAPS TO BUY/PREP.** Coastal cliff road, fishing-village docks at night, beached ship /
sea-cave ruin.

**⚠️ CONFIRM BEFORE RUNNING:** is Willa's sister **Mira** the same Mira as **Petal's mother**
in Hollowmere? If yes — that's a family scene *before* they leave in S3, and this road gets

## 🎪 THE WANDERING MARKET *(Nya's road · curiosity bait)*

**FINDING IT.** A rumor-triangulation mini-challenge: three sightings that can't all be true.
The trick — the market walks the **fey-seam**, camping where fairy-rings bloom. (Hollowmere's
own overnight rings mean it has been *close*.) Cracking this teaches the party to read the
seam — a world-mechanic they keep forever.

**THE GROUND.** Truce-law: no blade drawn on market ground, enforced by something unseen and
instant — show it once (a cutpurse mid-grab is simply *outside*, on his back beyond the
boundary-stones, oddly calm about it). Impossible stalls. And the **relic-stall**: salvage
from severed towns — Thornmere millstones, **Sallow Ness lantern-glass**, Farrowfield festival
ribbons. Someone is stripping the dead anchor-sites, and the market doesn't ask.

**THE SISTER & THE DECODE.** Quill, for her price: **one truth the party has never said
aloud** — their choice of speaker. Aim the confession engine at the table and wait.

**THE BEATS.**
- **The guild:** a spotter recognizes Nya. Truce-law holds — he just buys her a honey-sweet
  and says, **"The ledger's still open."** If the table wants the fight: his hired knives
  (2–3 + the spotter, who flees) wait past the boundary-stones at moonrise.
- **Sarranthia:** a fey goods-stall refuses her coin, the keeper frightened: *"Your credit's
  no good here — your guarantor's gone quiet."* 🔒 The patron-silence, made public for the
  first time. 🔒
- **Thane:** inside the market the brass compass **spins true and steady down** — the market
  itself sits on the seam. Also: memento-shopping paradise.

**THE SET-PIECE — moonrise departure.** The market *moves* at moonrise. Stay aboard — 🔒 it's
bound for Farrowfield 🔒 — or step off before the boundary-stones. A choice with a clock, not
a fight.

**MAPS TO BUY/PREP.** Night market / carnival grounds, moonlit field with standing
boundary-stones.
