## Campaign motifs (the threads that braid)

1. **Missing fathers.** Parker's father is Fey and arc-relevant.
   Chelsea's Nya never knew her father — possibly the source of her
   sorcery. If Jenna leans the same way for her Warlock, this becomes
   the central campaign refrain: *who gave us what we can do, and where
   did they go.*
2. **Bonds vs. severance.** Every NPC sits somewhere on this axis.
   Yelvyne (preserve), Wenneth (sever — mercy), Halve (sever — agenda),
   Belva (no opinion, just wants her village whole).
3. **What burns must be tended.** The Lantern Circle's creed. Echoes in
   Bahamut paladins (Cory's tempered-blade-not-gilded-idol read on
   faith). Echoes in Wenneth's tree dying slowly while she hides it.

- **Name:** still pending (suggested: elven name privately, road-name
  publicly)

- **Saves:** DEX, INT · **Feat:** Lucky
- **Expertise:** Sleight of Hand, Stealth
- **Backstory (Parker's words):**
  - Grew up in a secluded elven forest. Mother + stepfather, loving home.
  - **Real father disappeared when very young, no one ever explained.**
  - Left home early; works as a guide / escort, sometimes dangerous routes.
  - Wants purpose beyond survival.
  - Quiet, observant. Hard to trust, loyal once given. Values freedom.
  - Cares about: mother, stepfather, little sister, childhood friend

  - The severance of the Hollowmere shrine on Saturday will hit Parker
    in his body — nausea, vertigo, and a brief sense of a *door
    closing on someone he doesn't quite remember.*
  - Whoever-is-doing-the-severing is, somehow, connected to where
    Parker's father went. Long-arc payoff.
- **Saturday hooks:**
  - Belva calls him by an elven name he hasn't heard since he was a
    child — *one* of the mementos in his pack belongs to her village,
    and she recognizes the carving.
  - During Halve's recruitment beat: Halve says one word in Sylvan that
    only Parker's father called him. Don't translate it at the table.

### 4. Dragonborn Paladin — *Cory* (PC: Creed Ashmantle)

- **Class:** Paladin 1 · **Race:** Dragonborn · **Background:** Noble
- **Saves:** WIS, CHA · **Feat:** Skilled
- **Alignment:** Lawful Neutral
- **God:** **Bahamut** *(confirmed)*
- **Backstory (Cory's words):**
  - Born in a radiant temple; rose through priesthood young.
  - Booming voice, unwavering discipline. Inspired awe.
  - Divine power first answered as *warmth* — healing, blessing,
    shielding.
  - **Uncovered corruption** in the high clergy; spoke out; was
    branded heretic, stripped of vestments, cast out.
  - The god did not abandon him. The church did.
  - Now a paladin **bound by oath, not by institution.**
  - Walks the world as blessing AND warning. Aids temples that serve
    the people; dismantles those that rule them.
  - Kneels in prayer not for forgiveness but for clarity.
  - Wants to restore his own faith in people; prove not everyone is
    self-interested.
  - Flaw: lack of trust.
  - **Plays the lute** (background color).
- **DM-set canon:**
  - The temple Cory left was a **Bahamutan** temple corrupted from
    within. The god is fine; the institution was the rot.
  - His liege (House Ashmantle) sent him to Hollowmere to investigate
    Lantern-Eve. The liege has interest in the severances — agenda
    unknown to Cory.
  - Cory respects the gods but holds mortal clergy to a *harsher*
    standard than any sinner.
- **Saturday hooks:**
  - Yelvyne clocks his "Bahamutan bearing" the moment he walks into
    the shrine. She extends professional courtesy.
  - But: Cory's church-corruption history means he will *judge*
    Yelvyne and the Lantern Circle based on what they actually do for
    the people of Hollowmere — not on her credentials. The moral
    choice at session's end will land in him as: "Is this priest
    worthy of my sword arm, or my judgment?"
  - During Halve's recruitment beat: Halve mentions, by name, the
    high cleric Cory exposed. Halve says it with respect. Cory has
    to decide what that respect means.

## NPC extras (beyond the in-app card)

### Master Halve (the recruiter)

- **The big trick:** he calls out one detail per PC that *only that PC
  would know.* Order of cuts on Saturday:
  - **Drow Warlock:** something about her patron (the silence she's
    about to feel)
  - **Parker:** the Sylvan word his father called him
  - **Nya:** her birth name OR Death's Shadow (Chelsea picks; default
    both)
  - **Creed:** the name of the corrupt high cleric Creed exposed
  - **Willa:** a verse from a Lantern Circle text she's read but
    shouldn't have access to as an outsider
- **He leaves the loft BEFORE combat.** PCs returning find his cloak
  on the chair, old coin stacked on Belva's bar. No body, no scent.
- **Suspicion at session end: 100. Reveals: 0.**

### Wenneth (Dryad)

- The corrupted Dryad in Saturday's combat is **Hellbrim** — a friend
  of Wenneth's. Wenneth recognizes her. Read in Wenneth's voice in the
  PCs' heads during combat:
  > *"That is Hellbrim. We grew together for two centuries. Do not
  > hate her for what is no longer her."*
- Wenneth's slow-burn corruption arc begins Session 1. She wilts when
  the shrine severs. Each subsequent session she changes physically
  unless the PCs find a way to halt it.
- **Hidden tie-in to Parker:** Wenneth may have known Parker's father.
  Don't reveal Session 1. Hold for Session 3 or later.

### Belva

- Her **scarred ear** is the story she won't tell. Reserve for a later
  session when a PC presses hard enough.
- The old coin Halve paid in is from a **dead kingdom** Willa might
  recognize from her Sage reading.

### Priestess Yelvyne

- After combat, she offers the **Lay-of-Lanterns** ritual to reseal
  the shrine for one more night. If Hayley's player wants Willa to
  participate (Healing Hands as the catalyst), it adds narrative
  weight but mechanical impact is DM's call.
