## Master Halve — full backstory

**Pronunciation:** *HAHV.* Silent L, long A. Like "behalf" minus the
"be." Crisp consonants, soft V, almost swallowed.

### The history

~300 years ago, in a region long since renamed and forgotten, was
**Veilglass.** Its Feywild bond was the strongest in the world. The
silver light over Veilglass burned so bright the village had no nights
— only a permanent dusk. Children born during that light were *changed:*
split shadows, eyes that didn't track right, things said in their sleep
that came true. The Feywild was too close. The cost was paid in bodies.

Halve was one of those children. His mother was a high priestess of the
Veilglass Lantern Circle (the order that maintained the bonds). His
father was a Feywild being who came through the bond, married into the
community, and stayed. **Halve is half-Fey.**

In Halve's young manhood, a faction of priest-philosophers argued the
bond was killing the region and pushed for deliberate severance. His
mother fought them. The dissidents won — by force. They performed the
**first deliberate severance in recorded history.**

The backlash killed Halve's mother. His father was pulled back through
the bond at the moment of severance, never seen again. Halve, anchored
on both sides, was the only survivor in the close perimeter. The snap
pulled him in two directions and **did not break him** — but did not
leave him whole, either.

After the severance, Veilglass healed. Slowly. The corrupted children
stopped being born. The Feywild withdrew to a safe distance. The land
became ordinary. Beautiful in a quiet way.

The priest-philosophers were right.

Halve has been alive ever since. **He doesn't age** — half his soul went
out with the Feywild and is somewhere else, leaving the half that
remains in a kind of stasis. He has been **a man halved** for three
centuries.

### What he believes

> *"The bond is a parasite. Every region where it persists, the Feywild
> bleeds in and the mortal world rots out. I have seen this pattern five
> times in my life. The only thing that has ever worked is severance.
> The only thing."*

He's not lying. He's also not provably right. The campaign's central
tension is that his thesis MIGHT be true — and the PCs will never get a
clean answer.

**Kept deliberately OPEN (2026-07-15):** whether he's right, whether
he's rationalizing, or whether **something speaks through him.** He is
gone from Hollowmere and recruits from afar. Do not pin him down.

### Why he chose these five PCs

Halve selects targets based on **fey-adjacency.** A clean severance
needs people with one foot on the Material side who can still be heard
by the Feywild while it withdraws.

- **Drow Warlock (Jenna).** Her patron is fey-adjacent. The patron-bond
  itself is the relevant tie.
- **Wood Elf Rogue (Parker).** Half-Fey paternally. Halve may not know
  Parker's father was an earlier recruit of his.
- **Nya (Chelsea).** Her sorcery comes from a fey-touched paternal line.
  Halve suspects but hasn't confirmed.
- **Creed (Cory).** No fey-tie at all — the Material-side anchor the
  others stand on. (The old "Halve tipped Creed's investigation" idea
  is CUT — Creed found the corruption himself.)
- **Willa (Hayley).** Aasimar. Celestial-blooded — an **unusual
  resonance with the light.** (No keystone/vessel framing — see
  `parking-lot.md`.)

### The "wrong hand" (OPEN — Hellbrim is CUT)

**Hellbrim is cut entirely** (see `parking-lot.md`). Wenneth turned

What remains committed: the cipher, once decoded, hints at **an

## How severance actually works — mechanic

The bond between the Material Plane and the Feywild is **metaphysical**,
but every region has a **physical anchor** — a shrine, an oak, a stone,
a spring. The Hollowmere anchor is the **Lantern Shrine** (and
secondarily Wenneth's Chapel Oak). The shrine isn't the bond — it's the
*handle* the bond holds.

**The bond cannot be severed by force.** You can burn the shrine down
and the bond persists; another anchor will form. The bond can only be
severed by **negotiation** — somebody has to *speak* to the Feywild and
ask it to withdraw. The Feywild has to *consent.*

That requires three things working together:

1. **A speaker.** Someone half-anchored — one foot on each side. Halve.
   Only a handful of half-fey beings in the world fit this. Halve is the
   only one currently doing it.
2. **Witnesses.** Mortals with enough fey-adjacency that the Feywild
   recognizes them as "people who count" on this side. Their presence
   forms a resonant circle. The five PCs are this.
3. **A focal object.** Something physical that resonates with the bond.
   Usually the local shrine. Halve also carries his Veilglass anchor
   token — he pairs his token with the shrine for two anchors instead of
   one.

### The three phases

| Phase | When | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| **Snap** | Lantern-Eve, ~9-10 PM | Halve's ritual asks the Feywild to withdraw. The Feywild signals consent by extinguishing the shrine-flame. From the PCs' perspective: light goes out, Belva drops her tankard, Wenneth screams. **The bond is closing, not closed.** |
| **Retraction window** | ~10 PM until dawn | The Feywild pulls back slowly. The bond is ancient and tangled; untangling takes hours. **The bond can still be re-asserted from the mortal side during this window.** Corrupted fey appear — essence too old, too rooted, or too entangled to retract. The severance unmakes them mid-pull. |
| **Dawn lock** | Sunrise | Withdrawal completes. The bond is permanently gone. No counter-ritual works after this point. |

### Why Wenneth turns

Halve's ritual pulls **retractable** fey-essence home. What cannot
retract — essence too old, too deeply rooted in the place it loves —
stays behind, severed from its source. **The severance unmakes it, and
what is unmade becomes a corrupted creature.**

So most of Hollowmere's fey (the unseen, the deep-grove) went home
safely. Wenneth — three generations woven into this village — and the
blossom-seller's small glamour could not let go in time. **The
severance unmade them. Nothing pre-seeded it.**

From Halve's view: he saved ~70% of the local fey from corruption that
would have eventually consumed all of them. From Yelvyne's view: he
created the corruption by severing prematurely. **Both views are
coherent. Neither is provably right.**

## The three paths — RESOLVED IN SESSION 2 (kept for reference)

> **What they chose:** the party **relit the shrine** — closest to
> Path A, executed as their own improvised act, with the stated logic
> *"we can re-sever it later if needed."* **Live moral debt:** they
> overrode the victim's dying wish, and they believe severance is
> reversible. Neither belief has been tested.

### Path A — Reseal (Yelvyne's offer)

> *"We catch the light before it leaves. The bond holds."*

**Result:** Lay-of-Lanterns succeeds. Bond reasserted. Shrine relights.

**Cost:**
- **Wenneth and the corrupted fey stay corrupted permanently.** They
  were too damaged to retract; reasserting the bond doesn't fix them.
  They become ongoing threats in the grove.
- **The bond comes back tainted.** Whatever was wrong with Hollowmere's
  bond (the decay Halve was responding to) is still there. In 5-10
  years (campaign-time), Hollowmere will face a worse severance crisis
  with more corrupted fey.
- Halve will eventually return, more methodical, less polite.

### Path B — Let it sever (Wenneth's plea)

> *"Let it go. The Feywild does not need a leash."*

**Result:** No counter-ritual. Retraction completes at dawn. Bond is
permanently gone.

**Cost:**
- **The corrupted fey are stranded forever.** They live as monsters in
  the woods around Hollowmere. The PCs may have to put them down in
  later sessions. Mercy killings.
- **Hollowmere becomes ordinary.** No more silver-light festivals. No
  more Feywild miracles. The town loses something culturally and
  economically.
- **Yelvyne breaks.** Her purpose was the bond. She may leave the
  village. May not survive the year.
- Halve will eventually approach the party for the NEXT region. They've
  passed his test.

### Path C — The wild card

The PCs may try to do something unscripted. The Feywild is in
deliberation during the retraction window — they could attempt to
**negotiate directly** with it, instead of choosing between Halve's
withdrawal and Yelvyne's full reassertion.

This is the campaign's potential third path — the one Halve thinks is
delusional and Yelvyne is too institutional to imagine. Possibilities:
- A partial withdrawal — the bond retracts from corrupted areas but
  stays in healthy ones
- Ask the Feywild to heal itself first
- Ask Wenneth (in her dying clear moment) what *she* wants

Not guaranteed to work. **You, the DM, decide what's possible.** The
door is open. This is the option that grows into the campaign's main
thrust if the PCs find their way to it.

### The setting fact: the bright world hates the Drow

The fey-blending has made Veilmark **bright** — the shrines pour silver
light, the world has no true dark. For most peoples that's wondrous.
For the Drow it's hostile: a dark-adapted people with no shadow to live
in, physically and socially uncomfortable in the perpetual glow.

Worse — when shrines started going dark, the prejudice found its
scapegoat. *"Creatures of the dark. Of course the Drow want the light
gone. Of course they're behind the severances."* Across the campaign,
frightened towns blame the Drow for the severances. It is a **false
accusation** — the real severer is a half-fey man named Halve — which
makes the scapegoating tragic dramatic irony the players (who know the
truth) will feel keenly.

### The irony at Sarranthia's core

Sarranthia is the one person at the table who **most needs the bonds to
hold.** Her warlock power flows from a patron *inside the Feywild.*
Severance cuts that bond — and her magic dies with it. The world
assumes the Drow wants the dark; the truth is she is defending the
light harder than anyone, because the light is her lifeline.

She keeps her **patron's identity secret** — not for mystery's sake, but
survival: a Drow with a *visible* tie to the Feywild, in a world primed
to blame Drow for Feywild trouble, is a lightning rod.

### How it plays at the table

- **Saturday's moral choice becomes a vise for her.** The session ends

  *preserve* — and cannot say why without naming her patron. The most
  pro-preservation voice in the room is the one who can't explain
  herself. Pure roleplay tension; costs the DM nothing to set up.
- **Halve's recruitment is a personal knife.** Halve recruits
  fey-adjacent people for clean severances. Sarranthia's fey-adjacency
  *is* a patron severance would kill. He is asking her to help cut the
  branch she stands on. Canon: **Halve knows, and considers her power
  acceptable loss** — consistent with his 300-year certainty. When she
  realizes it, it's a betrayal with her name on it.
- **Scapegoat danger is world-level, not party-level.** Let the *world*
  distrust her; keep the other PCs (five first-timers) out of it early.
