## 🌿 THE GILDED BURR — Pib Thistlewhistle (the fey shop: mystery + curses)

### Mysterious — resolve on use (roll where noted)
- **Sealed Fey Vial** (40 gp) — unlabelled potion. On a drink, roll **d8**:
  1. **Bane** — 1d4 poison + DC 12 Con save or *poisoned* 1 hr.
  2. **Shrink** — as *reduce*, 10 min.
  3. **Fey glow** — skin shimmers; disadvantage on Stealth 1 hr (cosmetic).
  4. **Boon** — heals 2d4+2 HP.
  5. **Candor** — can only speak the truth, 10 min.
  6. **Grasshopper** — jump distance ×3, 10 min.
  7. **Fey-sight** — see invisible creatures 30 ft, 10 min.
  8. **Euphoria** — advantage on Cha checks, disadvantage on Wis, 1 hr.
- **Wishpenny** (25 gp) — flip *before* a roll that matters, then roll **d6**
  (penny is spent after): 1–2 misfortune (that roll has disadvantage), 3–4 the
  penny just warms (nothing), 5–6 fortune (that roll has advantage). One use,
  then it goes dull and ordinary.
- **Fey-Touched Preserves** (sold at Bramble & Crust, but fey-made) — see there.

### CURSED — looks great, has a bite (curse hidden until it bites)
- **Ring of the Open Hand** (150 gp) — *worn.* Truth: the wearer cannot hold
  onto coin. Whenever they could spend, tip, gift, or gamble gold, DC 13 Wis
  save or they do so generously (overpay, tip lavishly, hand a coin to a
  stranger). They feel *good* doing it. **Break:** *remove curse*, or a single
  act of genuine, costly selflessness that the ring "approves." Comedy + a slow
  gold drain; great on whoever hoards.
- **The Laughing Mask** (100 gp) — *worn.* Advantage on **Performance**; curse:
  the wearer can't stop quipping — **disadvantage on Stealth and Deception**,
  and on any nat-1 social roll they blurt an inconvenient truth. Pairs with the
  unnamed lutenist for pure comedy. **Break:** *remove curse*, or a sincere,
  unfunny heartfelt moment (DM's call).
- **Sweetdream Locket** (60 gp) — *worn to sleep.* Grants a full long rest's
  benefit even in rough conditions; curse: each night roll **d6**, on a **1** the
  sleeper murmurs a secret into the dark and something fey *listens* — a
  dream-spy, a visitor at the treeline, a whisper that reaches the Weaver.
  Escalate the Arc-2 fey plot with this. **Break:** *remove curse.*

### Clean magic (no catch)
- **Bottled Firefly Lantern** (15 gp) — hands-free soft light 15 ft, 1 hr per
  shake. Utility.
- **Charm of the Borrowed Grin** (120 gp) — 1/day, advantage on one Cha
  (Persuasion or Deception) check.
- **Fey-Touched Plum Jam** (5 sp) — 1/day, eat for 1d4 temp HP.

## 🥧 BRAMBLE & CRUST — Muddwyn Bramblecrust (consumables that do things)
- **Potion of Healing** (50 gp) — 2d4+2 HP. Staple.
- **Hearthfire Cordial** (40 gp) — 1 hr resistance to cold; once, remove 1
  level of exhaustion.
- **Festival Honeycakes** (3 gp/dozen) — per cake: 1 HP + advantage on the next
  Persuasion with a commoner. Social/heal.
- **Cask of Comfort** (45 gp) — 2d4+2 HP, but disadvantage on the next Dex
  check (tipsy). Comic heal.
- **Fey-Touched Preserves** (75 gp) — **mysterious.** On a taste, roll **d6**:
  1 +1d4 temp HP · 2 advantage on the next save · 3 *speak with animals* 10 min
  · 4 cast *minor illusion* once · 5 a vivid taste-memory reveals a small clue
  (DM's gift) · 6 sneeze glitter (cosmetic, funny).
- **Everwarm Hearthbrick** (30 gp) — warms a camp; the party ignores cold
  exposure through one night.
- **Traveler's Bramble Loaf** (2 gp) — eat before trouble: advantage on the
  next save vs fear.
- **The Legendary Meat Pie of Thistledown** (500 gp, aspirational) — once, a
  long rest completes in half the time (4 hrs). Impossibly good.

## Running notes
- **Loot bias (DM style):** steer the good finds toward the less bought-in
  players — not Nya. The cursed comedy items land best on whoever leans in.
- **Mysteries:** never tell players the odds. Let them buy the gamble; roll in
  the open or in secret as you prefer.
- **Curses:** don't announce them. Let the item behave; the reveal is the fun.
  All three break with *remove curse* if a player finds it.
- **Prices** are set on the cards; edit any at app → campaign → 🏪 Shops.
