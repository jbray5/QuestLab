# Session 1 — Arrivals at The Hearth — 🗄 HISTORICAL (played 2026-05-23)

> **Superseded.** What actually happened is logged in `the-severance.md`
> → Session Log. Lore referenced here may since have been CUT — see
> `parking-lot.md`.

> The first hour of the night. Designed to give five brand-new players a
> 3–4 minute entrance scene each, introduce the room organically, then
> pivot into the **Letter Reveal** and the rest of Act 1.
>
> Each PC arrives separately. Each entry below has:
> 1. **A short read-aloud** — the sensory establishing shot for that PC
>    when they cross the threshold (~30 seconds at the table).
> 2. **The arrival scene** — Belva + Wenneth + optional banter, with
>    multiple beats so the player has 3–5 things to react to.
>
> **Suggested order** (swap to match your players' comfort):
> Thane → Creed → Willa → Nya → Sarranthia.
> Principle: warm/easy first, dramatic last. Put the shyest player
> second or third (they get to watch the template, then go).

---

## Before the first PC walks in — set the room

> *(Out-loud to all five players, before anyone enters in character.)*

> The Hearth is warm. Woodsmoke, mead, roasted pumpkin, the slap of
> mugs on long tables. The lutenist in the corner is fighting one
> chord — not winning, but grinning about it. Belva is behind the
> bar, two pencils stuck in her gray-streaked hair, slamming a
> tankard down with the flat of her hand to punctuate a joke nobody
> quite heard. A woman with leaf-ribbons in her hair sits on a stool,
> slow and barefoot, telling another joke at half-speed. Outside the
> windows, the Lantern Shrine pulses silver across the rooftops. The
> festival is tomorrow. Tonight is the hush before it.
>
> One by one — you arrive.

## 1. Thane (Parker) — the warm welcome

### Read (as he enters)

> The latch lifts cool under your gloved hand and the door swings on
> a hinge older than you are. Heat hits first — woodsmoke and roasted
> pumpkin and the slap of warm air against a face that's been cold
> since Aldermarch. Then the smell of cider; then the sound of a
> lutenist losing a fight with one chord. The room is full but not
> crowded — long tables, the slow clink of mugs, low candles in iron
> sconces. Something in your chest eases a little. And then — quieter
> than the rest of it — you catch a thread of scent your blood
> remembers: *rain on warm stone.* There is a woman with leaf-ribbons
> in her hair sitting on a stool at the bar. She is barefoot. She is
> looking at you.

### The arrival scene

**Belva, clocking him from across the room** (loud enough to carry):

> *"Ohhh — finally, someone with dust on 'is boots! Y'come a long
> way, love. Sit y'self down — mead or cider?"*

*(Let Parker pick a drink. If he freezes, Belva's already turning
back to wipe a glass — no pressure.)*

**Wenneth catches him**, raising her wooden cup in his direction.
Soft Irish lilt at 70% speed, gaze drifting a beat off-axis:

> *"Eli'va."* (one beat) *"Oh — one of the between-folk. Sit, sit.
> The wine — is better than it has any right to be. Belva is a
> wonder."*

*(Eli'va — Sylvan for cousin. Don't translate. If Parker asks:
"That's just what we call each other, love. The road-folk.")*

**Belva returns, sliding his drink down warmly:**

> *"Don' encourage 'im, Wen. He's only just walked in."* (Wink at
> Parker.) *"What's brought y'to Hollowmere on Lantern-Eve, love?
> Pretty night for it. Y'eatin'?"*

**Open hook (if Parker stalls)** — a farm-lad two tables over leans
across, having heard "Lantern-Eve":

> *"You here for the festival, friend? Or just passin'? Belva's
> pumpkin pies are worth the road if y've never been."*

**Beats Parker can react to:** 4 — Belva's welcome, Wenneth's Sylvan
greeting, Belva's drink-and-question return, the farm-lad.

## 2. Creed (Cory) — the tease

### Read (as he enters)

> You duck the lintel — they always build doors for smaller people —
> and step into a noise you have forgotten in a long time: a room
> of strangers being kind to each other. Pies, mulled cider, the
> clack of a knitting needle, somebody losing badly at the lute. A
> child runs past your knee carrying a stick that is also a sword;
> the stick salutes you as it passes. A halfling behind the bar is
> mid-laugh, hand still raised from slapping the wood. You did not
> come here expecting to be welcomed, exactly. You came expecting to
> be *received.* You realise the difference now.

### The arrival scene

**Belva, eyebrows up, not spooked** (halflings have seen taller; she
also runs a tavern):

> *"Well now — that's a lot of you to fit in one chair, love. The
> benches're stout though, you'll be fine. Mead, cider, or somethin'
> warmer? It's a cold night out there."*

**Belva, sliding his drink over:**

> *"Y'eatin'? There's pumpkin pie, an' the cook's outdone herself if
> I do say. Festival's tomorrow — half the village is sleepin' here
> in spirit tonight."*

**Wenneth, from her stool, head tilted, calling across at half-speed
without quite getting up:**

> *"House Ashmantle."* (One full beat. She lets the name sit.)
> *"Your people used to send envoys to the shrines, back when.
> Before they decided profit was — more interesting."*

She says it lightly — teasing, not accusing. Like she's complimenting
him for being the kind of person who walked away from all that.

**Belva chimes in from behind the bar without turning around**, the
way only an old gossip can:

> *"Wenneth, leave 'im be — 'e's hardly through the door. Plenty of
> time to insult his people once he's had two drinks."*

*(To Creed:)*

> *"Don't mind 'er. She's been alive a long time and she remembers
> every single thing that ever annoyed 'er. Last week she was still
> cross about a man what stole a candle in 1972."*

**Optional — the lutenist's hopeful approach** (use if Cory looks
like a player who'd enjoy it; Creed plays the lute):

> *"You play, friend?"* (Nodding at the case on his back.)
> *"Lantern-Eve's tomorrow. I'd kill for some company up here."*

*(Cory can accept, decline, defer. Belva rescues if needed: "Maybe
later, love — the festival's long.")*

**Beats Cory can react to:** 5 — Belva's welcome, Belva's food
question, Wenneth's tease, Belva's affectionate scolding of Wenneth,
the lutenist's invite.

## 3. Willa (Hayley) — being seen

### Read (as she enters)

> It smells nothing like the sea. That is the first thing — warm
> air, sweet woodsmoke, the cooking that takes all day. The Hearth's
> fire is bright enough that you blink for a second; or maybe it is
> a different brightness, you couldn't say. A halfling at the bar
> looks up at you with the unguarded warmth of someone about to call
> you *"love."* A child running past stops dead, looks at you for
> two full seconds with a face you can't read, and then loses
> interest entirely and runs on. Welcome to the inland world, then.

### The arrival scene

**Belva looks up.** She doesn't know why she's softening, but she is.

> *"Well now."* (One beat.) *"Y'carry the light with you, love.
> Mead, or cider? Cider's mulled — better night for it."*

**If Hayley is sarcastic about being "noticed"** (very Willa), Belva
laughs:

> *"Oh hush, love, I say it to everyone who walks in fresh-faced."*
> (One-beat pause, deadpan.) *"...mostly."*

**The Welsh-elder grandmother in the corner**, knitting, half-moon
spectacles, looks up as Willa passes — and **nods,** once, slowly,
the way you'd nod at a person you knew long ago and weren't
expecting to see. Doesn't say anything. Goes back to her knitting.
*(DM-only: she has never met Willa before. She just knows.)*

**Wenneth, on her stool, watches Willa one beat too long, then looks
down at her wooden cup. Doesn't speak yet.** *(DM-only: the dryad
senses the Aasimar. She is filing Willa away.)*

**Belva returns with Willa's drink AND a small wooden bowl of
something hot — pumpkin soup, she didn't ask:**

> *"On the house, love. Y'look like y've been on the road longer
> than y're sayin'. Eat. Tell me — what's the world look like out
> past Aldermarch these days? Hearin' less from down that way than I
> used to."*

*(That's a real question. Belva is fishing for news. Hayley can
answer truthfully, embellish, or deflect — all good.)*

**If Hayley leans into sarcastic-deflective banter:**

> *"Oh, I LIKE you. Stay all winter, love — we'll get on
> famously."* (Slap of the hand on the bar.)

**Optional open thread** — a farm-lad on his way to the privy pauses
at her table:

> *"...sorry. You look like somebody I've seen — but I haven't, I
> don't think. Sorry."*

*(He moves on, embarrassed. DM-only: he's seen her in a dream he
doesn't remember. Future-session thread.)*

**Beats Hayley can react to:** 5 — Belva's softened welcome, the
grandma's nod, Wenneth's silent watch, Belva's news-fish, the
farm-lad's near-recognition.

## 4. Nya (Chelsea) — the hook

### Read (as she enters)

> The door's swing-back catches your shoulder and you step in before
> you decide to — which is how you have been entering rooms since
> you were thirteen. Bright. Loud. The smell of a kitchen that has
> not stopped working all day. You note three exits, two windows, a
> stairwell to a loft, and a halfling behind the bar with eyes like
> a woman who has counted you the moment you came in. A lutenist has
> just nailed a chord and looks alarmingly pleased about it. Nobody
> is looking at you. Everyone is looking at you. You have done this
> before.

### The arrival scene

**Nya steps up to the bar. Gives a name.**

**Belva pauses mid-pour.** Just one beat — but enough. Her eyes
flick to Nya's face. She finishes the pour, sets the tankard down
for the customer, turns to Nya:

> *"Funny."* (One beat.) *"Th'letter that came for you used a
> different name."*

*(Polite. Not accusatory. As if pointing out the weather.)*

> *"...Wha'll y'drink, love? Mead, cider, or somethin' that don't
> ask questions back?"*

**WAIT.** Let Chelsea react however she wants — laugh it off, claim a
mistake, ask whose letter, freeze, lie, threaten. **Belva absorbs
whatever it is.** That's the move — she's signalling: *I see you. I
don't care. Drink your cider.*

**If Nya asks about the letter:**

> *"Twine-bound. Sealed in silver wax. Came through this bar three
> nights back, ran out by a village kid the same night. Y'must've
> got it on the Silverway."*

**If Nya lies about the name:**

> *"'Course, love."* (Slow nod.) *"Mead it is."*

*(She's accepted the lie. Or has she? Chelsea won't know.)*

**Across the room, Wenneth — mid-joke with no audience — squints
over without breaking her sentence:**

> *"...you smell like a longer road than your boots suggest, love."*

*(Goes back to her joke without waiting for an answer.)*

**The lutenist starts up a new song just after Belva's "different
name" line** — a soft little tune, deliberately not paying attention
to the bar. **The room is pretending not to listen.** That's the
texture: Hollowmere lets you be whoever you want, but it *clocks*
you.

**If Chelsea freezes,** the grandmother in the corner calls across
without looking up from her knitting:

> *"Y'lookin' for a chair, dearie? One free here, if y'don't mind
> the company of a slow knitter."*

*(An out. Take it or don't.)*

**Beats Chelsea can react to:** 5 — Belva's mid-pour pause, the
letter line, Wenneth's drift-by observation, the lutenist's polite
cover, the grandma's rescue chair.

## 5. Sarranthia (Jenna) — the cinematic close

### Read (as she enters)

> The door pulls heavier than it should. You step across the
> threshold and the candles in the wall sconces gutter for half a
> beat — not from a draft, but as if *remembering.* A small,
> telling silence reaches across the room ahead of you — the silence
> of a room in which most of the people have never seen one of your
> kind, and the rest are old enough to have lived through a story
> about one. It is not hostile. It is the silence of decent people
> taking a breath before they decide how to be decent. A child's
> eyes go round; a parent gently turns her face away. A halfling at
> the bar finishes pouring a tankard before she looks up, and when
> she looks up she looks at you the way she looks at anyone.

### The arrival scene

**Belva, the absolute professional, does not pause her pour.** She
doesn't even look up. She finishes the tankard, sets it down,
*then* looks up. She gives Sarranthia exactly the same eye contact
she gave Thane:

> *"Mind those two."* (Slight tilt of the head at the locals who
> stared.) *"They stare at anyone whose name they haven't learned
> yet. They'll be on you for your life story in a week if y'stay.
> Drink, love? Mead, cider, somethin' stronger?"*

**Belva returns with her drink and one quiet extra:**

> *"On the house. Welcome to Hollowmere."*

*(Belva pauses one beat before "Hollowmere" — like Yelvyne does.
It's a village tic; most who live here have absorbed it without
realising.)*

**Wenneth, on her stool, has not looked at anyone all night.** Now
she looks. Direct eye contact with Sarranthia. No breath taken.
Then, with the soft Irish lilt and the tiny mid-sentence delays:

> *"Sit. The dark — is fine company."*

She raises her cup in a small toast. Goes back to her own thing.
**That was the entire welcome.** The room's silence breaks — the
lutenist plays a new chord, the locals look down at their drinks,
the festival noise resumes.

**Then — privately, only to Jenna.** Lean in low at the table, or
slide her a folded note. Her patron speaks for the first time
tonight:

> *"The light here is old. Older than your pact. Be careful what
> you promise tonight."*

*(Let her sit with it. Don't ask her to share with the table.)*

**Optional banter** — the same farm-lad who spoke to Parker walks
past Sarranthia's table, hesitates, decides honesty is better than
caution, and says:

> *"...I've never met one of your people before. Welcome. Sorry —
> I don't know if I was meant to say anything. I'm just glad
> you're here."*

He shuffles off, embarrassed at himself. **The kind of small, decent
thing that tells Jenna: this is not the wider world out there. This
is just a village.** Lands harder than any speech.

**Beats Jenna can react to:** 5 — the silence, Belva's smooth
recovery, Belva's on-the-house, Wenneth's direct toast, the patron's
private warning (+ the farm-lad's brave hello).

## After all five — the Letter Reveal *(revised — Belva as dispatcher)*

### What Belva knows (DM-only)

- Three nights ago the hooded scholar (Halve) rented her loft, paid in
  old coin "that don't tarnish."
- He gave her **five sealed letters** — twine and silver wax, with
  destinations along the Silverway.
- Belva had them run out by village kids that same night. Pip, the
  baker's son, ran two of them himself.
- She does **NOT** know what's inside. Sealed wax.
- She has been quietly **waiting** to see who arrives — without knowing
  which five.

### During the arrivals — she plays it cool

Each time a PC walks in carrying or near a twine-bound letter (visible
at belt, in hand, on a table) her eyes flick to it for a half-second.
**She doesn't tip her hand.** If you want to make this a noticeable
beat for any PC, drop one line:

> *"Belva's eyes go to your belt pouch for a half-second. She
> doesn't say anything."*

She'll wait until all five are seated and settled.

### The reveal — her move

She wipes the bar clean, picks up a tray of refilled drinks, comes
around with it, and parks herself beside the closest PC table —
loud enough for all five to hear, warm enough that it doesn't feel
like a confrontation:

> *"Right. The five of you."*
>
> *(One-beat pause. She sets the tray down.)*
>
> *"I been waitin' on you for three nights. Didn't know which five,
> mind — but five letters went out from this bar three nights past,
> twine an' silver wax, an' here y'are. Five letters in, five
> strangers out, an' nobody told me a thing."*
>
> *(She gestures at whichever PC is closest, gentle:)*
>
> *"Which one's yours, love? An' whose hand was on the seal? I'll
> wager it's the same hooded scholar fella what rented my loft.
> Paid in old coin that don't tarnish. Hasn't come back down since
> this morning. Bit odd."*

### Then step back

She's just handed the players:
- **A reason to talk to each other** — shared sender, same source-tavern.
- **A specific timeline** — three nights ago, from this bar.
- **A name to ask about** — the hooded scholar in the loft.
- **An anomaly** — he hasn't come back down.
- **An object of interest** — *old coin that don't tarnish.* Willa-bait
  on a high INT check: Veilglass mint, ~300 years old.

The party self-assembles. Hold your hands. Watch.

**If a PC asks the runner who brought the letter:** **Pip**, the
baker's son, ran some of them. He's at home now — can be chased later
if they want. He just delivered; he was paid a copper. Belva sent him.

## After the reveal — the next ~20 minutes (warmth)

This is the part new DMs always under-cook. **Don't.** The whole
emotional weight of the severance comes from the players loving
Hollowmere first. Slow down:

- The lutenist plays something slightly wrong, on purpose, and grins
  about it.
- Wenneth wanders over with her wooden cup, leans on the back of a
  chair, asks where each PC is headed after Lantern-Eve. Doesn't
  fully look at any of them. Calls Thane *"cousin"* once and moves on.
- Belva remembers what they drink before they ask twice.
- A villager bursts in laughing about a goat that got into the
  chandlery.
- **Willa's Lantern-Circle sigil moment** — when someone reads their
  letter aloud, Hayley auto-knows the sigil at the foot. Let her
  info-dump to the table. (Auto-success with Sage.)
- The pumpkin pie arrives.

**Don't ask for actions.** Just paint. The players will start asking
*you* questions — about the festival, about Wenneth, about the
hooded traveller upstairs. When they do, you've handed them the
wheel.

### Mention the shrine THREE times across the hour

The players need to register the silver light before it dies.

1. **Early** — *"Outside, the shrine pulses silver. The lutenist's
   chord wobbles. Festival folk wander past the windows."*
2. **Middle** — *"A villager pushes the shutter open for a second.
   The silver light spills in like cold water. The villager closes
   it again."*
3. **Just before Halve enters** — Belva offhand: *"Always brightest
   on Lantern-Eve, mind. Get used to it."* She doesn't know what's
   coming.

## Cheat-sheet — rescues, props, hooks

### The Belva Rescue (use any time a PC freezes)

> *"Y'awful quiet, love. Cider's fine, isn't it?"*
> *"Wha's the road been like? Heard wolves out Aldermarch way."*
> *"You eatin'? Pumpkin pie's just come out."*

### Standing props in the room (fill any silence)

- **The lutenist** — fighting one chord, then nailing it, then losing
  it again.
- **The child with the stick-sword** — running past in loops; salutes
  Creed.
- **The Welsh-elder grandmother** with knitting + half-moon spectacles
  — nods at Willa silently, available as a rescue chair for Nya.
- **The pair of farm-lads** at a long table, arguing gently — the
  bolder of the two is your "open hook" lad (Parker, Sarranthia).
- **An old fisherman** down from the brook with cold hands, sipping
  cider he's not paying for. Belva tolerates it.

### Hooks planted in the arrivals (use later)

- **Thane** — Wenneth's *"Eli'va."* (Kin recognition. DM-only: fey
  kin.)
- **Creed** — Wenneth's *"House Ashmantle"* tease + the lutenist's
  duet invite.
- **Willa** — the farm-lad's *"I've seen you somewhere I haven't."*
  Open thread.
- **Nya** — the *"different name"* beat + the grandma's rescue chair.
- **Sarranthia** — the patron's private warning + the farm-lad's
  brave welcome.

### Then Halve enters

When the energy peaks — table laughing, someone invested in something
small — that's your cut.

**Assume the five PCs are clustered at one table by now** (the Letter
Reveal pulled them together). Halve approaches the cluster, sets his
untouched cup on the edge of their table, and addresses them as a
group. The five cuts are still delivered *to* individual PCs — but
the others can hear the words. The point is the *gap*: the target
hears the full weight; the others only catch an oblique sentence and
can guess. That gap is the threat.

**Opening line (to the table):**

> *"You came because I asked. That is the first decision you have
> made this evening. Notice it."*

(One-beat hold. He looks around the table — not landing on any one
face yet.)

**The five cuts** — delivered in order, with one quarter-turn of body
and eye contact between each. Lines verbatim; full delivery cues on
Halve's NPC card.

| # | To | Line |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Sarranthia | *"Your patron. She's been speaking to you since you were sixteen, hasn't she? Make a note of her voice tonight. You may want to remember it."* |
| 2 | Thane | *"Aelim."* |
| 3 | Nya | *"Nya Ashwyn. Forgive me — I should say it properly. Death's Shadow."* |
| 4 | Creed | *"[Cleric's name] still asks after you, by the way. He sends his regards. Such as they are."* |
| 5 | Willa | *"And the bond that was kept in the dark was the bond that lit the world."* |

**Closing line + exit:**

> *"There's something at the shrine that wants seeing. Walk over with
> the Priestess — I'll catch up."*

Then he climbs to the loft. The door clicks shut. He does not catch
up.

## Halve Q&A — "what's your game?"

If any PC interrupts during the cuts, Halve absorbs the question with
**one short answer** and continues. If they corner him after, he holds
two lines, then turns. **He never escalates.**

| If they ask… | Halve says… |
|---|---|
| Who are you? | *"A concerned scholar."* (Pressed: *"My name is Halve. The rest is for later."*) |
| Why are we here? What do you want? | *"Tonight is not for that. Tonight is for the shrine. You will see — and then you will decide."* |
| What's at the shrine? | *"Something that wants seeing. You will know it when you do."* |
| How do you know that? | *"I listen."* Or: *"It is my work to know."* |
| What's your game? | (Two-beat hold.) *"Tonight. The shrine. Walk over with the Priestess."* Turn. |
| You wrote the letters? | *"I wrote five. You answered five. That is a coincidence I will not insult by calling it one."* |
| Are you a danger to us? | *"If I were, you would not be standing. I asked you here precisely because I am not."* |
| What's the coin? | *"An older mint. Belva is quite right — it does not tarnish. The world is full of things that don't tarnish, if you know where to look."* |
| Take off your gloves. | *"I would rather not."* (No more.) |
| Are you threatening us? | *"I do not threaten. I observe."* |
| Will you come to the shrine with us? | *"I will catch up. There is something I must finish here first."* |
| You're hiding something. | *"I am hiding a great many things. So are you, all of you. We will manage."* |
| Sit down. | *"I prefer to stand. The night is short."* |
| What's upstairs? | *"A room. With a bed I haven't used. I have been thinking."* |
| Show your hand. | *"I do not have one to show. Tonight is about yours."* |

**If a PC swings, draws, or grabs:** see the **Combat Deterrent** on
Halve's NPC card. The room will go very quiet. Do not roll if you
can avoid it.

## Act 1 dice checks — get them rolling early

Call **2–3 of these per PC** across the hour. The point is **hands on
dice early** — not difficulty. DCs are deliberately low to moderate;
save the hard rolls for Act 3.

### Willa (Hayley)

- Sigil on a letter → **INT (History) — AUTO-SUCCESS with Sage.**
  *"Lantern Circle. You've read about them. Local order, anchor-shrines,
  very old. Most villagers don't know the name."*
- Silver light through the window → **DC 14 INT (Arcana).** Success:
  *"This isn't fey magic the way your books describe. It's older. The
  references all assume the bond was always like this — but this light
  has been here longer than the references have."*
- The old coin, if Belva surfaces it → **DC 13 INT (History).** Success:
  *"Veilglass. A region renamed and forgotten centuries ago. The coin
  shouldn't exist in this condition."*
- On Halve, watching him work → **DC 20 WIS (Insight).** Even on
  success: *"You can't read him. The harder you look, the less you see.
  That's a tell of its own."*

### Thane (Parker)

- On entry → **DC 10 WIS (Perception):** the loft door is ajar;
  someone's been up there. (Reward; easy.)
- On Belva while she plays it cool → **DC 14 WIS (Insight):** *"She's
  expecting you. She knew someone was coming through her door
  tonight; she just didn't know who."*
- On the lutenist → **DC 12 WIS (Perception):** he plays the same
  wrong chord every fifteen minutes, almost like a watch tick.
- Slip toward the loft stairs → **DC 15 DEX (Stealth).** Success: he
  gets to look. Fail: Belva clocks him. *"Mind yer own, love. That
  room's took."*

### Creed (Cory)

- On entry → **DC 12 WIS (Perception):** Belva has a hand-axe AND an
  iron poker stashed under the bar. (Veteran tell.)
- On Wenneth's House Ashmantle tease → **DC 13 WIS (Insight):** she's
  teasing kindly, not accusing. Saves him from over-reacting.
- Quiet prayer over his drink → **no roll.** Bahamut is quiet but
  present. Lets Cory feel his faith before it's tested.
- On Halve's cleric line → **DC 14 WIS (Insight):** the cleric is
  alive. Halve has *seen* him recently.

### Nya (Chelsea)

- Vs. Belva's "different name" line → **DC 13 CHA (Deception)** to
  lie convincingly. Belva accepts any reasonable lie. Beat the DC:
  she pretends not to see through it. Miss: she still pretends, with
  one more knowing glance.
- To get Belva to talk about the loft tenant → **DC 12 CHA
  (Persuasion).** Beat it: Belva surfaces the coin (briefly) and the
  timeline.
- To get Belva to let her UP → **DC 15 CHA (Persuasion).** She'll say
  no regardless — but on a success she offers to *"wake him up and
  ask. He doesn't like to be bothered."*
- Sleight of Hand on the coin → **DC 12.** Fail: Belva swipes it back.
  *"Oh no y'don't, love."*
- On Halve naming her real name → **DC 12 WIS (Insight):** he's not
  threatening. He's informing.

### Sarranthia (Jenna)

- At entrance, defuse the staring locals → **DC 12 CHA (Persuasion or
  Intimidation).** Beat: they go back to their drinks. Miss: the
  silence holds a beat longer.
