# Hollowmere — Village Gazetteer

> Layout reference for Veilmark / **The Severance**. Every area and
> building here is keyed to `hollowmere-map.svg` (same folder).
> Canon source: the adventure record *The Hearth and the Hollow* and
> `the-severance.md`. Entries marked ✦ are new — safe to flesh out or
> overwrite.

## At a glance

- **~120 souls.** A woodland village in a shallow green hollow, ringed
  by the old oak **Mistwood** and a circle of low hills.
- The Feywild presses close here. Mushroom fairy-rings appear overnight;
  the brook runs faintly silver after rain; the old leave milk-bowls on
  their windowsills without remembering why. Before the severance the
  whole hollow rests in a gentle silver-warm glow, even at midnight.
- The holy night is **Lantern-Eve** — the autumn festival, when the
  shrine burns brightest and the fey neighbours come to dance among the
  humans without disguise.
- **One way in, one way out:** the Silverway.

## The shape of the town

Hollowmere is strung north-to-south along the **Silverway**, the only
road. Travellers from the south crest the rim of the hollow and look
down into woodsmoke and lantern-glow — the whole village laid out
beneath them like something held in a bowl.

The land rises as you go **north**. The village's high point is **the
Pale**, a low hill crowned by the Lantern Shrine — visible from anywhere
in town, and on Lantern-Eve the brightest thing for miles. The **heart**
of the village is its middle: the Market Green. The **south** end, where
the road comes in, is the working edge — the Hearth, the smithy, the
first crofts. The **west** side drops toward the Maerin Brook and the
dug-in cottages of Burrow Row. The **east** holds the quieter lanes and
the older homes.

Everything is close. A child runs from the Hearth to the shrine in ten
minutes. That smallness matters on Lantern-Eve: when the light dies,
nowhere in Hollowmere is *far* from anything else.

## The areas

**The Mistwood (the ring).** Old oak forest, wrapped the whole way round
the hollow. Mushroom rings, deer, and — in ordinary times — a polite
population of pixies. It is the village's larder, its fuel, and its
wall. After the severance it becomes the direction *everything wrong
walks in from*.

**The Pale (the shrine hill — north).** The highest ground. Bare-topped,
grass and standing stone — half religious site, half village commons.
Children play here; couples climb it at dusk. Holds the Lantern Shrine
and, a stone's throw down the slope, Priestess Yelvyne's cottage.

**The Hollow & the Market Green (the heart — centre).** The flat middle
of the bowl, where the village does its living: market, festival,
gossip, the well. The Green is the stage for Lantern-Eve.

**Burrow Row (the humble west).** Ten low cottages dug half into the
western hillside, smoke-blackened and warm. The festival cooks, most of
the children, and the eldest villagers live here. In the climax this is
the "these are the people we are protecting" stake.

**The Lower Lanes (the east).** Older, quieter homes and the craft
households — nothing grand, but the families who have been here longest.

**The Maerin Brook (the west edge).** Runs the length of the village's
western side, faintly silver after rain. Drives the mill, waters the
crofts, and is the children's summer place.

## The buildings — keyed to the map

1. **The Lantern Shrine.** Seven moss-eaten standing stones in a ring on
   the crown of the Pale. At the centre: a clear basin of still water
   and a single hanging lantern fed by no oil and no wick. In silver
   light it is lovely and warm; after the severance the lantern is an
   empty husk, the basin black, and the stones hum *wrong*.
2. **Priestess Yelvyne's House.** A small stone cottage a short way down
   the Pale from the shrine — the steward lives close to her charge.
   Behind a loose hearth-stone she keeps a journal: notes on three
   *other* anchor-points that have failed in the last six months. (The
   clue trail into Adventures 2–3.)
3. **The Hearth — the tavern.** Two-storey timber-and-stone at the
   village's south edge, the first building travellers reach. Its
   forge-warm hearth is said to share a flame with the shrine. Belva
   tends the bar. Narrow doorways, stout shutters, second-floor windows
   — defensible, which is the whole point on Lantern-Eve. A hooded
   traveller paid for the upstairs room three days ago and has not been
   seen since; the room holds a cold cup of tea and a page of cipher.
4. **The Market Green.** A grass commons ringed by a low (3-ft) stone
   wall, with three festival lantern-poles and a wooden dais. The
   Lantern-Eve dancing happens here. In the climax it is the killbox.
5. **The Chapel Oak.** A massive oak older than the village, growing up
   through a small open-air chapel near the Green. **Wenneth**, the
   oak's dryad, has been the village's gentle confidante for three
   generations. After the severance she emerges from it screaming.
6. **Burrow Row.** (See areas.) Ten dug-in cottages on the west hill.
7. ✦ **Maerin Mill.** A timber millhouse on the brook, its wheel turning
   slow and silver. Grinds the hollow's grain; the miller's family is
   large and loud.
8. ✦ **The Chandlery — the lantern-wright.** Every lantern in Hollowmere,
   and every candle for Lantern-Eve, comes from here — the most
   important craft-house in a village named for its light. A natural
   place for the party to gather shrine-lore, or to resupply before the
   dark.
9. ✦ **The Smithy.** Near the south road. Tools, shoes, hinges, the odd
   blade. A villager-ally and a source of improvised weapons in the
   climax.
10. ✦ **The village well.** Just off the Green. Stone-ringed, deep,
    cold. The brook may run silver — the well water runs *clear*, and
    the villagers trust it more.
11. ✦ **The boneyard.** A small walled plot beside the Chapel Oak. The
    village has buried its dead in the oak's shadow for generations —
    which will matter, the night the fey of Hollowmere turn.
12. ✦ **Crofts & fields.** Smallholdings at the village's skirts —
    pumpkins, barley, the festival's food. The outermost crofts are
    closest to the Mistwood, and least safe after dark.

## After the severance — the town as a map of danger

The same map reads differently once the light dies:

- **The Mistwood** stops being a wall and becomes a *gate*. Everything
  hostile walks in from the ring — from every direction at once.
- **The Pale** is the objective: the shrine, the choice, Yelvyne's
  ritual. It is also the most exposed ground in the village.
- **Burrow Row & the outer crofts** are the soft targets — far from the
  Hearth's defensible walls, full of the people the party most wants to
  save.
- **The Hearth** is the fortress: narrow doors, shutters, the one
  building built like it expected a bad night.
- **The Silverway south** is the only evacuation route — 120 souls, in
  the dark, toward Aldermarch, with the Mistwood pressing both sides of
  the road.

## At the table — Session 1 movement

The night walks the party up the map, south to north:
**The Hearth** (Act 1 — arrival & recruitment) → **the Market Green**
(Act 3 — *Hold the Hearth*, the fight spills out of the tavern onto the
Green) → **the Pale / the Lantern Shrine** (the after-combat choice, and
Session 2's ritual). Keep the shrine *visible* all night — its light is
the clock.
